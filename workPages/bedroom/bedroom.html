This introduction assignment was the first one of the semester. I spent hours mulling over it, as I wasn't sure how to introduce myself into this honors English class. So, I stuck to what I knew, and wrote a little bit of freeform poetry, since the structure in the rubric for this assignment said we could be creative. I picked out parts of myself that I found significant, the parts that made me unique, and I wrote them all down for the world to see, before composing them into one of my favorite art forms. It was a little nerve wracking, since I didn't know anyone in this class at first, and I feared the rejection of others thinking I was pompous for writing a poem or oversharing on the first assignment. However, this fear was unfounded, as I came to find like minded friends in this class. Though more open-ended assignments like these were sometimes a bit of a hardship, I learned throughout the course how to use my own style to fill in the gaps that the rubrics left in my head.

Who I Am - Andrea Linder



I am an experience. Perceived by others and myself as somewhat strange, sometimes a friendly one, sometimes not so much. I am the experience of my parents meeting by chance through a double blind date. I am all of the people I have met in life and conversed with. I am all of the places I have been, and all of the songs I have listened to. I am a brain, made of gray and white matter, sitting inside a skeleton controlling a nervous system to type this post out right now. I am the topics that I could scream about to the open sky, and I am the late nights spent whispering callouts into my microphone for my team in various inconsequential video games. I am constructed out of jokes, flimsy and fleeting. I am my maternal grandma's doppelganger, coincidentally looking almost exactly like her and being named after her. I am the middle child, the bookworm. I am the thrill seeker of the family. I've leapt from heights where others would falter, and I've suffered the consequences of poor decisions. I'm a student, forever learning and seeking more information. I am a lover of all animals, seeking to understand feelings and find new experiences to further enrich my life and theirs. I am a friend, a sibling, an heir. I am American, born and raised in Arizona, though my ancestors hail from England and Northern Europe. I am a future hopeful medical student, hoping to heal others. I am like a prop plane, sometimes needing a push before I can fly on my own. I am the tens of thousands of hugs that I have given and received over my lifetime. These things aren't just parts of my life, fragments of memories as they may seem, as they're all parts of me. I am Andrea.
